Distribution Complexity & Geographic Alignment

Ferrero operates a strongly multi-channel distribution model across 25+ online retailers. Products are NOT Amazon-dominant - they sell across mass market grocery (Tesco, Sainsbury's, ASDA, Walmart, Target, Kroger), quick commerce (Flink, Picnic, Knuspr), specialist online (World of Sweets, Flaschenpost), and emerging DTC channels (Ferrero Shop Italy, Thorntons UK, Fannie May US).

UK & Ireland (~25-30%)

Exceptionally strong grocery presence: Tesco, Sainsbury's, ASDA, Morrisons, Ocado, Waitrose, Lidl, Aldi. This is eStore's strongest market - fast time-to-value.

Continental Europe (~20-25%)

Strong presence across Germany (active pilot), France, Italy, Benelux, Scandinavia. HQ in Luxembourg, operational HQ in Alba, Italy. Country offices in 50+ markets.

US (~35-40%)

Significant and growing through acquisitions (WK Kellogg, Ferrara, Wells). eStore's strength is European - US operations would require honest conversation about coverage and timeline.

Other Markets (~10-15%)

APAC, LATAM, MEA. Mexico PoC previously completed. Regional expansion creates future monitoring opportunities.

Key insight: Marie-Jeanne Matei (VP E-commerce US) has explicitly stated Ferrero's strategy involves "getting closer to retail partners" with retailer-specific programmes. The multi-channel, multi-market distribution creates exactly the complexity where DSA delivers highest ROI.
