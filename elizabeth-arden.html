Distribution Complexity & Geographic Alignment

Geographic breakdown: ~50% US, ~35% UK/Europe, ~15% Asia-Pacific/Other. Multi-channel, NOT Amazon-dominant. UK is reportedly Revlon's second-largest e-commerce market after the US.

Elizabeth Arden operates a genuinely multi-channel distribution model across mass retail (Walmart, Target), department stores (Macy's, Nordstrom, John Lewis), specialty beauty (Sephora, Ulta, Lookfantastic), pharmacy chains (Boots UK, CVS), and marketplaces (Amazon US/UK). This diversity creates real digital shelf monitoring complexity.

Strong UK high street presence through Boots, John Lewis, and Marks & Spencer. Good European footprint via Douglas (Germany), Notino (pan-EU), and Sephora (France/EU). 16+ country-specific DTC e-commerce sites add a content consistency challenge.

European Corporate Infrastructure

Elizabeth Arden has legal entities and operational teams in seven European countries: UK (London), France (Paris), Germany (Wiesbaden), Austria (Vienna), Denmark (Brondby), Spain (Madrid), and Switzerland (Geneva). This is not a US company with token European distribution - it's a genuinely multi-market European operation.

eStore alignment: European retailer coverage should be relatively strong given eStore's existing monitoring of major UK and EU beauty retailers. Key retailers like Boots, Amazon UK, Sephora, Douglas, Notino, and Lookfantastic are within eStore's infrastructure scope. US coverage will require honest assessment - lead with European strength.
