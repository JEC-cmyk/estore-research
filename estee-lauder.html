Objection Handling Principles

  • Never dismiss the objection: Every concern is valid from the prospect's perspective. Acknowledge first, then respond.
  • Be honest about limitations: Coverage gaps, US challenges, and implementation timelines should be acknowledged upfront. Trust is built through transparency.
  • Ask questions before answering: Understand the root concern. "What's driving that question?" often reveals more than the surface objection.
  • Offer flexibility: Pilots, phased rollouts, and regional starts reduce risk for the prospect.
  • Give easy outs: If the fit isn't right, say so. "We might not be the right fit for that specific need" builds credibility.
  • Reference their context: Use their stated priorities, public statements, and strategic initiatives to frame responses.
  • Focus on value, not features: What problem does eStore solve for them? Lead with that, not product capabilities.
