Common Objections & Responses

"We already use Stackline for digital shelf analytics" ▼

Response approach: Don't challenge Stackline directly - acknowledge their strength on Amazon and position eStore as complementary for the channels Stackline doesn't cover well.

Key points:

  • "Stackline is strong on Amazon - no argument there. The question is whether 35% of your revenue flowing through FDMC channels and 36% through international markets gets the same level of visibility."
  • "This isn't about replacing what's working. It's about whether there are gaps in non-Amazon and European coverage that leave blind spots."
  • "We've worked alongside other DSA providers at several clients - complementary toolsets often give the most complete picture."

Evidence: Stackline's published case study focuses explicitly on Amazon category insights and custom segments. No mention of FDMC, European retailers, or content compliance.

Redirect: Ask what visibility they currently have into Walmart, Target, Boots, Holland & Barrett, and ASDA performance. If the answer is limited, that's the gap.

"We evaluated eStore in 2024 and didn't proceed" ▼

Response approach: Acknowledge the history honestly and focus on what's changed since then - both at eStore and at GPN.

Key points:

  • "I know we connected back in September 2024 and things didn't progress - I'm not entirely sure why, and I'm not going to pretend otherwise."
  • "A lot has changed at GPN since then - new leadership, new strategic priorities, the Body & Fit divestiture shifting focus to retailer channels."
  • "eStore has also evolved significantly - particularly around actionability, AI-powered prioritisation, and European retailer depth."
  • "Given the changes on both sides, it might be worth a fresh conversation - no pressure if the timing isn't right."

What NOT to do: Don't be defensive. Don't assume you know why they didn't proceed. Don't dismiss their previous evaluation.

"How many of our retailers can you actually monitor?" ▼

Response approach: Be transparent about strengths and gaps. Lead with European coverage where eStore is strongest.

Key points:

  • "For your UK and European retailers - Amazon UK, ASDA, Tesco, Boots, Holland & Barrett, European Amazons - we have strong existing coverage and can deliver value within 4-8 weeks."
  • "For US mass retailers like Walmart and Target, we also have coverage."
  • "For specialist nutrition retailers like GNC and Vitamin Shoppe, I'd want to have an honest conversation about what's possible and what timeline looks like."
  • "Rather than overstate what we can do, I'd prefer to start with the markets where we can deliver immediate value and expand from there."

Positioning: Honesty about coverage builds trust. A phased approach (Europe first, then expand) is more credible than promising everything immediately.

"We're in the middle of a transformation - not the right time" ▼

Response approach: Respect the timing concern genuinely. Don't force urgency that doesn't exist.

Key points:

  • "Completely understand. Transformations create enough complexity without adding new vendor evaluations on top."
