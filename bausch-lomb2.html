"How many of our retailers can you actually monitor?" ‚ñº
Response

That's a fair and important question. For your Consumer Division products - eye drops, supplements, lens care solutions - we have strong coverage. We monitor CVS, Walgreens, Boots, and all major UK grocers. That's where LUMIFY, Blink, and PreserVision are sold, and we can start providing visibility there within weeks.

Evidence

We cover 94% of Tier 1 mass market retailers (17 of 18), including Amazon, Walmart, Target, CVS, Walgreens, Boots, Tesco, Sainsbury's. The gap is in specialist optical retailers where we have 0% coverage currently.

Reframe

Would it make sense to start with Consumer Division visibility where we can deliver immediate value, and explore specialist retailer coverage as a second phase?

"We already have a PIM system" ‚ñº
Response

That makes sense - most brands at your scale do. A PIM is essential for managing what you send to retailers. What we do is different: we show you what's actually live on retailer sites. Often there's a gap between what's in your PIM and what consumers see on CVS.com or Boots.com.

Evidence

We commonly see content inconsistencies between what brands publish centrally and what appears on retailer sites. Stock availability issues, incorrect images, outdated descriptions - these happen even with robust PIM systems because retailers don't always pull updates quickly.

Reframe

Think of us as the verification layer. Your PIM manages what should be there; we show you what actually is. Would it be useful to see where gaps exist between your published content and live retailer pages?

"We get data directly from Amazon through Vendor Central" ‚ñº
Response

Vendor Central is excellent for your own performance data. What it doesn't show you is what competitors are doing - how they're pricing, what content they're using, where they're winning buy box, how their promotional strategy is evolving. That competitive layer is what we add.

Evidence

Brands with strong Vendor Central usage often tell us they have a blind spot on competitive intelligence. You know your metrics well, but the external view - what's actually happening with competitor products on the same retailer pages - requires dedicated monitoring.

Reframe

We complement Vendor Central rather than replacing it. Your internal data plus external competitive data gives you the complete picture. Is competitive visibility something you're actively trying to solve?

Opal is a smart investment for connecting eye care practices directly with patients. What it doesn't cover is the third-party retail channel - what's happening on CVS, Walgreens, Amazon, Boots. Those retailers carry your Consumer Division products but aren't part of the Opal ecosystem.

Opal addresses B2B2C for the optical channel. But Consumer Division products like LUMIFY, Blink, and PreserVision flow primarily through pharmacy and grocery retailers that operate independently. That's a visibility gap that Opal wasn't designed to solve.

üìã Objection Handling Principles

General Approach

  • Never dismiss the objection - Acknowledge it as valid and reasonable
  • Use evidence and examples - Specific data is more convincing than general claims
  • Offer flexibility - Pilots, POCs, phased approaches reduce risk
  • Be honest about fit - Acknowledge limitations upfront; it builds trust
  • Ask questions to understand root concern - The stated objection may not be the real issue
  • Set realistic implementation timelines - Overpromising undermines credibility
