Alcohol e-commerce requires navigating multilayered regulations:

Competitive Category Dynamics Create Urgent Need

Oligopoly Market Structure

Global beer industry concentrated among three major players:

  • Anheuser-Busch InBev: 25-28% global market share, $59.77B revenue, dominant position creating defensive imperative for Molson Coors
  • Heineken: 12.9% global share, ~$30B revenue, strong European presence
  • Molson Coors: World's third-largest brewer, 23% North America share, 18% EMEA & APAC share, second-largest by volume in both regions

US Competitive Intensity

Despite industry headwinds (US beer down 4-6% in 2024), Molson Coors faces fierce competitive threats:

  • Constellation Brands: Highest share in US premium segment with Corona and Modelo brands threatening core business
  • Core Brand Performance: Coors Light, Miller Lite, Coors Banquet retained ~80% of 2023 share gains, gained nearly 2 share points Q3 2024 vs Q3 2022
  • Market Challenges: Americas segment net sales declined 11% Q3 2024, financial volumes down 15.6%, macroeconomic pressures limiting disposable income
  • Craft Beer Pressure: Craft category softness led to divestiture of underperforming brands (Hop Valley, Terrapin, Revolver, Atwater to Tilray August 2024)

E-commerce Growth Opportunity

Despite current challenges, e-commerce represents massive white space:

  • Current Penetration: Just 1% of beer purchases occur online (industry-wide 2019 data), only 3% of US alcohol sales via e-commerce
  • Consumer Awareness Gap: 35% of consumers still unaware beer can be bought online (2024), up from 47% awareness in 2021
  • Growth Projections: Global alcohol e-commerce reaching $36B by 2028 (IWSR), US CAGR 7% (2023-2027), forecasts ranging 7-31% CAGR depending on source
  • Penetration Trajectory: Industry experts predict 10% penetration within 5-8 years, up from sub-1% pre-pandemic
  • Molson Coors Performance: 21% B2C e-commerce CAGR (2021-2024) outpacing 18% category average, voted #1 supplier for e-commerce by distributor network

Category Trends Creating Urgency

  • Premiumisation Acceleration: Above-premium products growing faster than core, EMEA & APAC already 50%+ above-premium vs 21% in Americas
  • Health & Wellness Impact: Non-alcoholic beer growing 8% annually, Molson Coors' NA portfolio grew 89% in 12 weeks ending December 2024
  • RTD/Flavoured Beverages: Hard seltzer and flavoured categories requiring different digital shelf strategies than core beer
  • Online Purchase Behaviour: Above-premium mix 1.6X higher online vs offline, younger Millennials index 121 online, creating strategic channel for premiumisation goals

Objection Handling Principles

Core Approach

  • Never dismiss the objection: Even if it seems illogical, it represents a real concern to them
  • Ask clarifying questions: Often the stated objection isn't the real issue
  • Use evidence: Facts, examples, case studies are more credible than assertions
  • Offer flexibility: Pilots, phased approaches, customisation options
  • Be honest about fit: If they're genuinely not a good fit, say so

When to Walk Away

Sometimes the right answer is to acknowledge this isn't the right fit:

  • Retailer coverage is too low and they need immediate value
  • They're genuinely satisfied with existing solution and have no gaps
  • Budget simply isn't there and won't be for 12+ months
  • They don't have organisational readiness (too chaotic, no ownership)
  • Their expectations exceed what we can realistically deliver

In these cases: Be honest that it's not the right fit right now. Leave the door open for future conversations. They'll respect the honesty and may come back when circumstances change.
