Timing and Cadence

  • Don't send on Mondays (inbox overwhelm) or Fridays (weekend mode)

  • For Jenny specifically: wait until mid-February as she suggested

Personalisation Checklist

  • Mention specific initiatives or achievements where relevant
  • Acknowledge existing tools they use without being dismissive
  • Be honest about what eStore can and cannot do
  • Use British English throughout

Response Handling

  • Positive response: Move to scheduling a call. Keep it brief and focused.
  • Timing pushback: Acknowledge and offer to reconnect at their preferred time.
  • "We use e.fundamentals": Acknowledge their success, position as complement not replacement.
  • No response after 3 emails: Stop the sequence. They may come back when timing is right.

Objection Handling Principles

Core Approach

  • Never dismiss the objection: Even if it seems illogical, it represents a real concern
  • Ask clarifying questions: Often the stated objection isn't the real issue
  • Use evidence: Their own data and success (e.fundamentals ROI) is more credible than our claims
  • Offer flexibility: Pilots, phased approaches, specific market focus
  • Be honest about fit: If we genuinely can't help, say so

Specific to Molson Coors

  • Always acknowledge e.fundamentals success - don't pretend we don't know about it
  • Position as complement, never as replacement
  • Focus on ASDA Xpert (activation) and CEE (geographic expansion) as differentiated opportunities
  • Be honest about US - not our strength, their existing tools are probably fine there
  • Reference leadership changes as context for fresh evaluation, not pressure

When to Walk Away

Sometimes the right answer is to acknowledge this isn't the right fit:

  • They're genuinely satisfied with existing tools and see no gaps
  • Budget won't exist for 12+ months and there's no pilot pathway
  • They need immediate full global coverage (we can't deliver this)
  • The internal champion (Jenny) has moved on or lost influence

In these cases: Be honest that timing or fit isn't right. Leave the door open for future conversations. They'll respect the honesty and may come back when circumstances change.
