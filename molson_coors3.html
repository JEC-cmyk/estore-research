Why This Brand Fits eStore's Sweet Spot

Distribution Complexity

Molson Coors operates a true hybrid model that validates digital shelf monitoring needs. They sell through mass market retailers (Amazon, Walmart, Target), alcohol specialists (Drizly, Total Wine, BevMo), grocery chains (Kroger, Tesco, Sainsbury's), and their own DTC platforms (Ship and Sip, Revl). The three-tier alcohol distribution system adds regulatory complexity that multiplies the challenge of maintaining consistent product presence.

Portfolio Breadth Creates Monitoring Burden

With 80+ brands including Coors Light, Miller Lite, Blue Moon, Carling, Staropramen, ZOA Energy, Simply Spiked, and Naked Life spirits, they face enormous SKU proliferation. Recent launches (2024-2025) include Blue Moon Non-Alcoholic, Madr√≠ 0% lager, ZOA new flavours, and Simply Spiked summer variants - each requiring Day 1 optimisation across 30+ retailers simultaneously.

Competitive Pressure

The beer oligopoly creates intense rivalry. Constellation Brands (Corona, Modelo) is gaining premium segment share. AB InBev remains the dominant player. This competitive intensity means digital shelf excellence is a competitive weapon - being outranked or out-of-stock on Amazon or Walmart directly translates to lost sales to rivals.

Digital Maturity & Strategic Alignment

Molson Coors has invested heavily in digital infrastructure including Salsify PIM (350,000+ assets), Domo analytics, Shopify Plus DTC platforms, and multiple digital shelf tools. Their stated "transform faster" mandate from leadership suggests openness to solutions that accelerate digital commerce performance. However, this also means they have established vendor relationships to navigate.

Those dynamics apply to beer on Amazon, Walmart, Drizly just as they do to other CPG categories."

Point to their own validation

"More importantly, you've already validated digital shelf ROI in alcohol - 16:1 with e.fundamentals. That wasn't because e.fundamentals had alcohol expertise. It's because optimising content and search works regardless of category. We're offering to extend that approach across more retailers."

Objection Handling Principles

Core Approach

  • Never dismiss the objection: Even if it seems easily addressed, it represents a real concern to them
  • Ask clarifying questions: Often the stated objection isn't the real issue
  • Use evidence: Facts, examples, their own data are more credible than assertions
  • Offer flexibility: Pilots, phased approaches, proof of concept before full commitment
  • Be honest about fit: If we're genuinely not the right solution, say so

When to Walk Away

Sometimes the right answer is to acknowledge this isn't the right fit:

  • They're genuinely satisfied and have no gaps we can fill
  • Existing vendor contracts prevent consideration for 12+ months
  • Budget is frozen with no realistic path to reopening
  • No internal champion and no willingness to explore
  • Their expectations exceed what we can realistically deliver

If walking away: Be honest that timing or fit isn't right. Leave the door open. They'll respect the honesty and may come back when circumstances change. Better than forcing a bad-fit deal.
